VEX Robotics Competition

VEX Robotics Competition (VRC) is a robotics competition where middle and high school students design, build, and program robots to compete at regional, state, national, and international competitions. At tournaments, each team will be randomly assigned an alliance partner, and they will work together to achieve the highest ranking. After the qualifying matches, the top-ranked teams can select their permanent alliance partners until the team capacity for the elimination rounds is reached. Afterwards, the permanent alliances compete for the final championship, and the top two teams qualify for the State Competition.

The current game for the 2020 - 2021 season is Change Up.

General Rules:

Below are some of the general rules for the VRC Competition. Each year, there may be certain exceptions and/or additional rules.

  • Each team is partnered with another team, which makes up an "alliance." In any match, two alliances, one designated as the "red" and the other as the "blue" alliance, compete against each other.
  • The robot must be within the dimensions of an 18-inch cube.
  • The robot cannot contain any software or material that is now allowed by VEX Robotics.
  • The playing field is a 12x12 ft area consisting of foam tiles bordered by polycarbonate dividers.
  • Each match starts out with a 15-second autonomous period, where the robot moves solely based off of pre-programmed instructions.
  • The "driver control" period starts after the autonomous period. The designated driver(s) of the team manually control their robots for 1 minute 45 seconds.
  • Up to 3 competitors per robot are allowed to be in their "alliance station" or "driver pits," an area surrounding the field.
  • The main goal is to score more points than the opposing alliance.
  • The alliances are not allowed to go into the other alliance's protected zone.
  • Removing any game objects on the field before the game begins will result in a disqualification, as will intentionally damaging any game objects.
